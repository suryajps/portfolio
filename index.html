Surya Prabhakaran





Surya is a sustainability specialist who approaches built environments as climate mediators that should work with nature, and not fight against it. At KieranTimberlake, she combines scientific research and data analysis to understand how materials, carbon, energy, and light interact with building systems. She embraces the challenge of creating evidence-based strategies that serve both the users and the environment. Her work spans material health assessments, building performance analyses, visual comfort studies, and embodied carbon accounting (LCA) for projects ranging from historic retrofits to new construction. Starting from her upbringing in Chennai, and continuing on her professional and educational journey through Bangalore and Philadelphia, she has gathered a deep understanding of built environments in diverse climates, and their impact on occupant comfort. These environments determine whether you can work and sleep comfortably, whether you can breathe clean air, and whether opening a window helps or hurts. Beyond occupant comfort her current focus lies in balancing high-performance building design with sustainable and ethical building material sourcing with the goal of improving social health. Between running daylight models and carbon calculations, she can be found planning her next hiking adventure, or exploring the greater Philadelphia area by bike. She welcomes conversations about material supply chains, environmental justice, and tips on how to improve her latte art.
