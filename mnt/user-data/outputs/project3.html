Spatial Analysis: CO2 Emissions for Drone vs Truck Deliveries

Year: 2021 Type: Spatial Analysis & Environmental Modeling Collaboration: with Atharva Ranade

This comprehensive spatial analysis project investigates the environmental impact of delivery methods by comparing CO2 emissions between drone and truck deliveries in urban and suburban contexts. Using advanced geospatial analysis techniques, we analyzed delivery networks in Manhattan (high-density urban) and Yonkers County (suburban) to understand how delivery mode efficiency varies with urban density and infrastructure.

The study employs shortest path algorithms, network analysis, and spatial clustering to model realistic delivery scenarios. By analyzing factors such as traffic patterns, delivery density, flight restrictions, and infrastructure constraints, we provide data-driven insights into the environmental tradeoffs between emerging drone delivery systems and traditional truck-based logistics.

Project Components

Background Research

Literature review and narrative analysis of existing research on delivery methods, drone technology capabilities, and urban logistics systems.

Manhattan Analysis

High-density urban analysis using OSMNX network analysis, shortest path algorithms, and DBSCAN clustering to model delivery patterns in complex urban environments.

Yonkers County Analysis

Suburban context comparison examining how lower density and different infrastructure patterns affect the relative efficiency of delivery modes.

Results & Conclusions

Comprehensive comparison of CO2 emissions, discussion of findings, and recommendations for future implementation and research directions.
