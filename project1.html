Sanctuary of Datum

Year: 2020 Type: Academic Collaborators: Sung Di, Abinayaa Perezhilan Instructors: Dorit Aviv, Zherui Wang

The storage "clouds" behind endless scrolling on Instagram, streaming on Netflix, and processing ChatGPT queries have a physical presence. Data centers consumed around 4.4% of U.S. electricity in 2023, and are projected to reach 6.7-12% by 2028. By 2030, they are projected to contribute 1.4% to global greenhouse gas emissions. How do we ensure that digital preeminence has minimal environmental impacts? How can hot and dry climates be used to our advantage to power these data centers?

This project explores a site located in Phoenix, AZ, where large diurnal temperature swings can be leveraged to incorporate evaporative cooling. The project investigates effects of solar radiation and night flushing on shaping the building geometry. The design evolves from a cone-shaped geometry inspired by Harran Domes in Turkey, then aggregates the geometry in various sizes and orientation testing for minimal solar heat gain on the building envelope. The geometry is further optimized for maximizing exposure to the night sky for night-cooling potential. Materiality is driven by adobe structures and their physical properties such as thermal mass and thermal conductivity.
