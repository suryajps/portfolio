Visualizing Transportation Carbon - Drone vs Truck

Year: 2020 Type: Academic Collaborators: Atharva Ranade

This project visualizes the environmental impact of package delivery methods. Using geospatial analysis techniques, we map truck routes and drone paths in urban (Manhattan) and suburban (Yonkers County) contexts. The study employs shortest path algorithms, network analysis, and spatial clustering to model realistic delivery scenarios. Acknowledging that traffic patterns, delivery intensities, and flight restrictions also play a crucial role in understanding actual impact on the carbon footprint. The study proposes a visual method to understand environmental tradeoffs between emerging drone delivery systems and traditional truck-based logistics.

Project Components

Background Research

Review and adoption of research methodology analyzing delivery methods, drone technology capabilities, and urban logistics.

Manhattan Analysis

High-density urban analysis using OSMNX network analysis, shortest path algorithms, and DBSCAN clustering to model delivery patterns in complex urban environments.

Yonkers County Analysis

Suburban context comparison examining how lower density and different infrastructure patterns affect the relative efficiency of delivery modes.

Results & Conclusions

Visualizing distances and GHG emissions, summary of findings, and limitations in current practice.
